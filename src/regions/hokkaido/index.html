Spring: Late Blooms & New Beginnings

As the deep snows of Hokkaido begin to melt, the countryside comes alive. Unlike the rest of Japan, cherry blossoms here bloom in late April to mid-May, making it a peaceful option for travelers chasing the sakura season.

Wander lakeside paths around Lake Toya, where steam rises from nearby volcanic vents, or enjoy uncrowded blossom spots in smaller towns and farms. Rural spring in Hokkaido is quiet, clean, and deeply refreshing.
