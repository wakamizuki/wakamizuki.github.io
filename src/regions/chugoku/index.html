Chugoku Countryside – Travel Guide to Rural Japan

Discover Rural Chugoku: Sand Dunes, Islands, and Local Life

Chugoku is a region of dramatic coastlines, mystical landscapes, and deep history! Explore the famous Tottori Sand Dunes, wander through the ancient streets of Tsuwano, and visit the sacred island of Miyajima. From the iconic floating torii gate to the scenic Shimanami Kaido cycling route, Chugoku’s countryside is full of natural wonders and authentic experiences.

Explore Chugoku Countryside

Chugoku Countryside Highlights

  • Miyajima (Hiroshima): Sacred island with the famous floating torii gate of Itsukushima Shrine.
  • Tottori Sand Dunes (Tottori): Japan’s largest sand dunes and unique desert scenery.
  • Tsuwano (Shimane): Historic castle town known as “Little Kyoto of San’in,” especially famous for Taikodani Inari Shrine.
  • Kurashiki (Okayama): Beautiful canal district with preserved Edo-era warehouses.
  • Tsunoshima Bridge (Yamaguchi): Stunning bridge with turquoise waters and island views.
  • Shimanami Kaido (Hiroshima/Ehime): Scenic cycling route across islands and bridges.
