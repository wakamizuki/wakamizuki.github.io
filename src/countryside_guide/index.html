Hakone & Surroundings

A classic getaway near Tokyo, Hakone offers the perfect mix of nature, culture, and relaxation. Cruise across the scenic Lake Ashi with Mount Fuji in the distance, visit the historic Hakone Shrine with its iconic torii gate on the water, and explore the volcanic landscapes of Owakudani, where steam rises from the earth. Art lovers will enjoy the open-air exhibits at the Hakone Open-Air Museum, while countless hot springs (onsen) provide a soothing retreat after a day of sightseeing.

Oshino Hidden Village

Step into a peaceful village near Mt. Fuji, famous for its crystal-clear spring water and traditional thatched-roof houses. This area, known as Oshino Hakkai, features eight sacred ponds fed by snowmelt from Mt. Fuji, offering stunning reflections of the mountain on a clear day. Explore the tranquil walking paths lined with local snack stalls, souvenir shops, and picturesque footbridges. You can also visit the Oshino Ninja Village, a fun and family-friendly spot where you can learn about ninja history and try hands-on activities. For those interested in culture and history, the Hannoki Bayashi Shiryokan (Heritage Center) displays traditional tools and daily life artifacts from old Japan. Whether you're after nature, culture, or just a quiet escape with great photo ops, Oshino Hidden Village offers a rich and relaxing experience.

Kamaboko Experience at Suzuhiro in Odawara

Discover the art of making kamaboko, a traditional Japanese fish cake, at Suzuhiro in Odawara. Learn about the history and craftsmanship behind this delicacy through interactive workshops and exhibits. Visitors can try their hand at making their own kamaboko and savor freshly made samples. The village also features a museum, gift shop, and a restaurant serving dishes highlighting kamaboko. It's a unique cultural and culinary experience perfect for food enthusiasts and families alike.

What Was a Sekisyo Checkpoint?

Discover the role of sekisyo checkpoints in Edo-period Japan. Learn how these historic barriers controlled travel, protected the shogunate, and shaped the journeys of samurai, merchants, and commoners along routes like the Tokaido. Explore preserved sites such as Hakone and Arai Sekisyo, and uncover the legacy of these fascinating checkpoints.
