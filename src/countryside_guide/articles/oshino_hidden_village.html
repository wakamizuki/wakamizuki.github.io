Hidden Gems of Oshino Village: A Japanese Countryside Escape

Explore Oshino, a hidden gem in the Japanese countryside near Mt. Fuji, offering crystal-clear springs, ninja adventures, and beautiful rural walking trails.

1. Oshino Hakkai

Overview:

Oshino Hakkai is a group of eight crystal-clear ponds formed by the snowmelt from Mt. Fuji, filtered through volcanic rock over decades. Surrounded by traditional thatched-roof houses, the area preserves the feel of old Japan.

Why Visit:

Walk along the wooden paths and peer into the ponds teeming with life. You can even taste the fresh spring water and experience the tranquil scenery.

2. Oshino Ninja Village

Overview:

A fun, interactive theme park where visitors can dress up in ninja costumes, try shuriken throwing, and explore trick houses.

Why Visit:

Ideal for families and groups, this attraction lets you step into the world of ninjas in an immersive and playful environment.

3. Shinnashogawa Promenade

Overview:

A peaceful walking trail along a clear stream near Oshino Hakkai, surrounded by seasonal flowers and cherry blossoms.

Why Visit:

It’s a perfect photo spot for catching the reflection of Mt. Fuji on the water. Best visited in the morning or evening light.

4. Mt. Fuji World Heritage Center (Oshino Annex)

Overview:

This educational center uses interactive exhibits to explore the geological and cultural history of Mt. Fuji.

Why Visit:

Enhance your understanding of the mountain before or after your visit, with hands-on experiences and short films.

Clear spring water and traditional thatched-roof houses at Oshino Hakkai.

Try ninja training and explore trick houses at the Oshino Ninja Village.

A serene riverside promenade with seasonal flowers and reflections of Mt. Fuji on the water.
