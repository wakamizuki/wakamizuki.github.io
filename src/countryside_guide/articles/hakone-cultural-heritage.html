Explore Hakone's Timeless Heritage: A Journey into Japan's Countryside

Discover the rich cultural and historical landscapes of Hakone, a gem in the Japanese countryside. From sacred shrines nestled in the mountains to centuries-old travel routes, Hakone offers a captivating blend of spirituality, history, and natural beauty. Experience a side of Japan where ancient traditions and breathtaking scenery come together.

1. Hakone Shrine

Overview:

Hakone Shrine is a historic Shinto shrine located at the foot of Mount Hakone, along the shores of Lake Ashi. Founded in 757, it has been a center of worship for centuries, attracting samurai, pilgrims, and travelers who sought protection and blessings for their journeys.

Connection with Hakone Mototsumiya:

While Hakone Shrine stands beautifully by the lakeside, its spiritual origin lies atop Mount Komagatake at Hakone Mototsumiya. Originally, pilgrims climbed the mountain to worship at this sacred summit shrine. Today, visiting both Hakone Shrine and Hakone Mototsumiya allows you to trace the path of devotion from lake to mountaintop.

Why Visit:

Stroll through the tranquil forest paths leading to the main shrine, admire the iconic torii gate rising from Lake Ashi, and soak in the spiritual atmosphere that has inspired generations of worshippers.

2. Hakone Mototsumiya Shrine

Overview:

Hakone Mototsumiya, meaning "original shrine," sits at the peak of Mount Komagatake. Established as the sacred origin of Hakone Shrine, it offers panoramic views over Hakone and Mount Fuji on clear days.

Connection with Hakone Shrine:

Hakone Mototsumiya serves as the inner shrine of Hakone Shrine. Historically, pilgrims would first ascend to Mototsumiya before visiting the main shrine by the lake. The two shrines together form a spiritual connection between the mountain and the lake, linking the earth with the heavens.

3. Hakone Old Road (Hakone Kyukaido)

Overview:

The Hakone Old Road, or Hakone Kyukaido, is a historic section of the Tokaido route that connected Edo (Tokyo) and Kyoto during the Edo period. Lined with towering cedar trees, stone pavements, and historical markers, this path offers a glimpse into Japan's feudal past.

Connection with Hakone Checkpoint:

The Old Road leads travelers toward the Hakone Checkpoint, an important security post that controlled traffic between east and west Japan. Walking this road today retraces the footsteps of samurai, merchants, and officials who once journeyed under strict inspection at the checkpoint.

Why Visit:

Wander through this atmospheric trail shaded by centuries-old cedars. Feel the echoes of history as you follow the path toward Hakone Checkpoint, passing historic teahouses and stone milestones along the way.

4. Hakone Checkpoint

Overview:

The Hakone Checkpoint was one of the most significant security posts along the Tokaido route during the Edo period. Established by the Tokugawa shogunate, it monitored travelers and goods moving between regions, playing a key role in maintaining control over the realm.

Connection with Hakone Old Road:

The checkpoint marked a critical juncture for anyone traveling the Old Road. After enduring the steep climb over the Hakone mountains, travelers would arrive here for inspection. The site today features reconstructed gates, guardhouses, and exhibits detailing its historical importance.

Why Visit:

Step back in time as you explore the meticulously restored checkpoint buildings and museum. Gain insight into the lives of travelers who passed through Hakone centuries ago, and enjoy panoramic views of Lake Ashi from the observation area.
