Explore the Japanese Countryside: Scenic Adventures in Hakone

Embark on an unforgettable journey through the Japanese countryside with our Hakone outdoor adventure guide. From the tranquil shores of Lake Ashi to panoramic views of Mount Fuji, discover the beauty, nature, and hidden trails that make Hakone one of Japan’s most scenic destinations.

1. Sengokuhara Susuki Grassland – Autumn Pampas Grass Fields in Hakone

Overview:

Sengokuhara is home to a vast field of susuki (Japanese pampas grass) that turns golden in autumn. This seasonal landscape draws photographers and nature lovers alike.

Why Visit:

Visiting Sengokuhara Susuki Grassland is like stepping into a sea of golden waves. The soft swaying of the pampas grass under the sunlight creates a mesmerizing scene, especially during sunset. Walk the gentle paths through the grassland for an immersive experience in Hakone's natural beauty.

2. Lake Ashi Cruise – Scenic Boat Ride with Mount Fuji Views

Overview:

Experience the beauty of Lake Ashi from the water by taking a sightseeing cruise. The lake is dotted with picturesque spots, including views of Mount Fuji and the surrounding mountains.

Why Visit:

A boat cruise offers a unique way to enjoy the panoramic views of the lake and Hakone's natural scenery. For a fun twist, board the Hakone Pirate Ship—a themed cruise popular with families and tourists. Cruises also connect key spots around the lake, making it convenient for exploring other attractions.

3. Hakone Ropeway – Aerial Views of Volcanoes and Fuji

Overview:

The Hakone Ropeway is an aerial cable car offering breathtaking views of Mount Fuji, Lake Ashi, and Owakudani's volcanic landscapes. Stretching from Sounzan to Togendai, it covers a scenic route over Hakone’s mountains.

Why Visit:

Riding the Hakone Ropeway is an unforgettable experience, letting you see Hakone from above. On a clear day, the sight of Mount Fuji towering over the landscape is simply stunning. The ropeway is also the best way to access Owakudani Valley, where you can explore volcanic activity up close.

4. Old Hakone Road – Historic Edo Path with Cedar Trees

Overview:

Old Hakone Road, also known as Kyu-Kaido, is a historic trail that once connected Tokyo and Kyoto during the Edo period. Walking this ancient stone-paved path lets you experience Japan’s rich history while surrounded by beautiful natural scenery.

Why Visit:

Among the highlights of Old Hakone Road is Cedar Avenue—a breathtaking path lined with towering cedar trees planted hundreds of years ago. Walking under the shade of these giant trees feels serene and timeless, offering a unique blend of nature and history. It’s perfect for peaceful strolls, photography, and escaping into nature’s calm.
