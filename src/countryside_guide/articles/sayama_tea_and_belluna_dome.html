Sayama Tea, Tokorozawa Park & Baseball at Belluna Dome: A One-Day Trip from Tokyo

Discover a unique day trip from Tokyo: travel from Shinjuku to the lush tea fields of Sayama, experience hands-on tea picking at Miyanoen Garden, and end your day with an exciting baseball game at Belluna Dome. This course blends rural charm, local flavors, and Japanese sports culture—all in one day.

1. Morning Departure: Shinjuku to Sayama Tea Fields

This model course is based on schedules and information as of August 1, 2025.

Overview:

Start your journey at Shinjuku Station. Walk to Seibu-Shinjuku Station and board the Seibu Shinjuku Line Express (bound for Hon-Kawagoe). Enjoy a direct ride through Tokyo’s suburbs into Saitama’s countryside.

Recommended Route:

  • 08:14 — Depart Shinjuku Station, walk to Seibu-Shinjuku Station
  • 08:25 — Board Seibu Shinjuku Line Express (bound for Hon-Kawagoe, 450 yen)
  • 09:17 — Arrive at Sayama-shi Station
  • 09:20 — From Sayama-shi Station East Exit, take Seibu Bus “Sayama 31” bound for Sayamadai Danchi (178 yen)
  • 09:38 — Arrive at Sayamadai Minami bus stop
  • Walk about 11 minutes to Miyanoen Garden
  • 09:51 — Arrive at Miyanoen Garden

Tips:

  • Check train and bus schedules in advance, especially on weekends or holidays.
  • IC cards (like Suica or PASMO) are accepted on trains and buses.
  • Express trains are direct and comfortable, but confirm if you need a reserved seat or can use regular seating.

2. Tea Picking Experience at Miyanoen Garden

Overview:

Arrive at Miyanoen Garden by 10:00 AM for a hands-on tea leaf picking event in one of Japan’s three major tea regions—Sayama. Dress in a traditional tea-picking outfit, stroll through vibrant tea bushes, and enjoy a countryside escape filled with laughter and fun. The garden is a little far from the nearest station, but the refreshing atmosphere and Sayama tea’s beauty benefits make it worth the visit.

Event Details:

  • Lecture on tea picking
  • Tea picking from the garden
  • Handmade tea making
  • Tasting of tempura tea leaves

Event Period & Fee:

  • Period: Early May to early November
  • Fee: 1,500 yen and up

Tips:

  • Reservation required: Admission is by reservation only. Please book in advance (email recommended).
  • Weather: Outdoor farm experience—may be canceled in case of inclement weather.
  • Language: Limited English support, but staff are friendly.
  • Best season: May to early June for the first harvest.

3. Afternoon Transfer: Miyanoen Garden to Tokorozawa Aviation Memorial Park

Access Details:

  • 13:08 Depart Miyanoen Garden (about 11 minutes on foot)
  • 13:19 Board Seibu Bus “Sayama 31” bound for Sayama-shi Station East Exit at Sayamadai Minami bus stop (178 yen)
  • 13:32 Arrive at Sayama-shi Station East Exit, walk to Sayama-shi Station
  • 13:46 Take the Seibu Shinjuku Line Express (toward Seibu Shinjuku, 220 yen) from Sayama-shi Station
  • 13:55 Get off at Kōkū-kōen Station, walk about 10 minutes from the East Exit
  • 14:08 Arrive at Tokorozawa Aviation Memorial Park

Tips:

  • Please check bus and train schedules in advance.
  • IC cards (Suica/PASMO) are accepted.

4. Enjoy Tokorozawa Aviation Memorial Park

Overview:

Spend a relaxing afternoon at Tokorozawa Aviation Memorial Park, which features vast lawns, the Aviation Museum, and cafes. Enjoy a picnic, stroll around, or explore the aircraft exhibits.

Recommended Points:

  • Learn about the history of Japanese aviation at the Aviation Museum
  • Take a break at the park’s cafes or shops
  • Enjoy seasonal flowers and nature

5. How to Get from Tokorozawa Aviation Memorial Park to Belluna Dome

Access:

  • From the west exit of Tokorozawa Aviation Memorial Park, walk about 20 minutes to Tokorozawa Station.
  • 17:00 — Arrive at Tokorozawa Station. Board the Seibu Ikebukuro Line (Semi-Express, bound for Hannō) from platform 5.
  • 17:06 — Arrive at Nishi-Tokorozawa Station (platform 3). Transfer to the Seibu Sayama Line (departing from platform 2), bound for Seibu-Kyūjō-mae.
  • 17:16 — Arrive at Seibu-Kyūjō-mae Station (platform 1), the nearest station to Belluna Dome.
  • From the main exit, walk about 2 minutes to Belluna Dome.

Tips:

  • Trains may be crowded on event days, so allow extra time for transfers.
  • IC cards (Suica/PASMO) are accepted for all train rides (fare: 188 yen).
  • Check train schedules in advance, especially for evening games.

6. Enjoy Watching Baseball at Belluna Dome

Overview:

Watch professional baseball at Belluna Dome! The stadium has a roof, so you can enjoy the game regardless of the weather. Try local gourmet foods and check out the fan merchandise. Experience the unique excitement of Japanese baseball cheering.

Recommended Points:

  • It’s best to purchase tickets in advance
  • Enjoy stadium gourmet food and limited-edition goods
  • Check out post-game events and fireworks
