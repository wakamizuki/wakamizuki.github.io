Hakone & Surroundings: A Gateway to the Japanese Countryside

Discover the perfect blend of nature and culture in Hakone and its surroundings. From the peaceful shores of Lake Ashi and the sacred Hakone Shrine to the dramatic volcanic landscapes of Owakudani and the open-air sculptures at the Hakone Open-Air Museum, this countryside getaway offers hot springs, scenic views of Mt. Fuji, and a refreshing escape into Japan’s natural beauty.

1. Hakone Shrine

Overview:

Hakone Shrine is located at the foot of Mount Hakone, along the shores of Lake Ashi. It is known for its iconic torii gate that stands in the water, creating a breathtaking view. The shrine is a popular spot for visitors to pray for good fortune and enjoy the surrounding nature.

Why Visit:

This peaceful shrine offers a serene atmosphere with beautiful walking paths through the forest. The iconic torii gate and the views of Lake Ashi and Mount Fuji make it one of the most photographed sites in Hakone.

2. Lake Ashi (芦ノ湖)

Overview:

Lake Ashi, also known as Ashinoko, is a scenic lake located in the Hakone area. The lake offers stunning views of Mount Fuji and is surrounded by lush forests, making it a great place for boat cruises, hiking, and sightseeing.

Why Visit:

Visitors can enjoy a boat cruise across the lake to take in the views of Mount Fuji. You can also enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking around the lake or visiting hot springs nearby. The lake is home to the Hakone Pirate Ship, a fun and popular attraction for families and tourists alike.

3. Owakudani (大涌谷)

Overview:

Owakudani (valley) is a volcanic valley known for its active geothermal activity. It is famous for its hot springs, sulfur vents, and the opportunity to try the famous "black eggs" that are boiled in the volcanic waters.

Why Visit:

You can take a ropeway up to the valley and enjoy stunning views of Mount Fuji, as well as experience the unique sulfuric smell of the volcanic area. The black eggs, which are said to increase your lifespan, are a must-try snack while visiting.

4. Hakone Open-Air Museum

Overview:

The Hakone Open-Air Museum is Japan’s first outdoor museum, featuring a wide collection of modern art displayed in an outdoor setting. The museum includes sculptures from both Japanese and international artists.

Why Visit:

The museum’s beautiful location among the mountains and gardens makes it a great place for both art lovers and nature enthusiasts. It's a peaceful escape with works by Picasso, Henry Moore, and many other famous artists. The museum also features the Picasso Pavilion and other interactive exhibits for visitors of all ages.

5. Hot Springs (温泉)

Overview:

Hakone is famous for its hot springs, and there are numerous ryokan (traditional inns) offering baths with views of the surrounding nature. Many of the hot springs are located in scenic areas with breathtaking views of Mount Fuji.

Why Visit:

Relaxing in an onsen with a view of Mount Fuji is one of the top experiences in Hakone. You can enjoy the therapeutic benefits of the hot spring water while surrounded by the beautiful nature of Hakone. Many ryokan also offer private baths for those seeking more privacy.
