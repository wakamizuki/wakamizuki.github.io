Lake Sugatami-no-Ike – Mirror Pond of Asahidake

Overview: Reflecting Hokkaido’s Highest Peak

Lake Sugatami-no-Ike (姿見の池) is a small, crystal-clear pond located near the upper station of the Asahidake Ropeway in Daisetsuzan National Park. On calm days, the pond perfectly mirrors the towering peak of Mt. Asahidake and the surrounding alpine scenery. The area is famous for its volcanic steam vents, wildflowers in summer, and vibrant autumn foliage.

Highlights and Nature

The pond’s name, “Sugatami” (meaning “mirror”), comes from its ability to reflect the mountain’s image. The area is easily accessible via a well-maintained boardwalk from the ropeway’s upper station (Sugatami Station). Along the way, you’ll pass volcanic fumaroles, alpine plants, and panoramic viewpoints. The pond is a sacred site for the Ainu people and a favorite for photographers and hikers.

*Ropeway operates year-round except for maintenance. The boardwalk to Sugatami-no-Ike is about 1.7km round-trip (easy, but weather can change quickly).

From the upper ropeway station, follow the well-marked boardwalk to Sugatami-no-Ike. The walk takes about 30–40 minutes round-trip. In summer, enjoy alpine flowers; in autumn, the pond is surrounded by brilliant foliage. Volcanic steam vents are visible along the trail.
Tip: Bring a jacket, as weather is often chilly and windy even in summer.

Access on Foot (From Ropeway Station to Sugatami-no-Ike)

The pond is a short, scenic walk from Sugatami Station (the upper ropeway station). The trail is mostly boardwalk and suitable for families and beginners.

Please stay on the designated paths to protect the fragile alpine environment.

Activity Information

  • Location: Sugatami-no-Ike, Daisetsuzan National Park, Hokkaido, Japan
  • Best Season: Late June–early October (hiking, reflections), late Sep–early Oct (autumn colors)
  • Trail Distance: ~1.7km round-trip from ropeway station
  • Difficulty: Easy (boardwalk, some steps)
  • Notes: Weather changes rapidly; bring warm, waterproof clothing. Stay on trails to protect nature.
