Shiretoko Five Lakes – Hokkaido’s Pristine Wilderness

Overview: Natural Beauty in Shiretoko National Park

Shiretoko Five Lakes (知床五湖, Shiretoko Goko) is a breathtaking natural attraction located in Shiretoko National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site on the Shiretoko Peninsula, Hokkaido. The five tranquil lakes are surrounded by primeval forest and offer spectacular views of the Shiretoko mountain range. Visitors can enjoy easy boardwalks and more adventurous ground trails, with opportunities to spot wildlife such as deer, foxes, and even brown bears. The area is especially beautiful in spring and autumn, making it a must-visit for nature lovers and hikers. For more ideas on what to see nearby, check out the "Find Recommended Sightseeing Spots Near Shiretoko Five Lakes" section below!

Nature and Wildlife

The Shiretoko Five Lakes area is renowned for its untouched natural environment. The lakes reflect the surrounding mountains and forests, creating stunning scenery in every season. The elevated boardwalk allows visitors of all abilities to enjoy the views safely, while the ground trails offer a closer look at the flora and fauna. Wildlife is abundant—keep an eye out for Ezo deer, red foxes, and a variety of birds. Brown bears also inhabit the area, so safety briefings and trail restrictions may apply during certain seasons.

Walking Trails at Shiretoko Five Lakes

Elevated Boardwalk: Open to all visitors, free of charge, wheelchair accessible, and offers panoramic views of the lakes and mountains.

Ground Trails: Require registration and a short lecture during bear season (late April–July, September–October). Fees apply. Trails may close if bears are active.

Please check the official website for up-to-date trail conditions and seasonal restrictions.
