Lake Toro – Canoeing & Nature in Kushiro Wetlands

Overview: Hokkaido’s Tranquil Wetland Lake

Lake Toro (塘路湖) is a serene lake in the heart of Kushiro Shitsugen National Park. Known for its calm waters and beautiful wetland scenery, it’s a top spot for canoeing, birdwatching, and relaxing in nature. The area is famous for its misty mornings and abundant wildlife, including Japanese cranes.

What Can You Do at Lake Toro?

Lake Toro is a paradise for outdoor lovers. Glide across the lake by canoe or kayak, join a guided tour, or simply stroll along the shore. The area is a haven for birdwatchers, especially in spring and autumn. Local shops offer canoe rentals and tours.

  • Rent a canoe or kayak and explore the lake
  • Join a guided canoe tour on the Kushiro River
  • Birdwatching: spot Japanese cranes, eagles, and more
  • Enjoy lakeside walks and photography
  • Relax at local cafes or try Smelt Ice-fishing (permit required)

Local Legend: The Misty Lake’s Spirits

Locals believe that the morning mist on Lake Toro is the breath of ancient spirits protecting the wetlands. Early risers may feel the gentle presence of these guardians as they paddle across the calm water.

Local saying:
「塘路湖の霧は大地の息」
Translation:
"The mist of Lake Toro is the breath of the earth."

Lake Toro Details

Lake Toro is suitable for all ages and skill levels. Local guides can help beginners enjoy canoeing safely. The area is peaceful and perfect for a relaxing day in nature.
