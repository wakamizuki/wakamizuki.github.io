Smelt Ice Fishing in Lake Toro – Winter Activity in Kushiro Wetlands

Overview: Experience Traditional Japanese Ice Fishing

Smelt ice fishing (ワカサギ釣り) is a classic winter pastime in Hokkaido. At Lake Toro (塘路湖), you can try fishing for smelt through holes drilled in the frozen lake, surrounded by the snowy beauty of Kushiro Shitsugen National Park. It’s a fun activity for families, friends, and anyone wanting to experience authentic Japanese winter culture.

What Can You Do?

After drilling a hole in the ice, drop your line and wait for a bite! Local guides provide all the gear and tips you need. Many visitors cook their catch right on the ice, enjoying crispy smelt tempura fresh from the lake.

  • Try smelt ice fishing with rental gear and local guides
  • Cook and eat your catch as tempura on-site
  • Enjoy the peaceful snowy scenery of Lake Toro
  • Warm up in nearby cafes or rest areas
  • Great for families, groups, and beginners

Local Legend: The Spirits of the Frozen Lake

Locals say the shimmering smelt beneath the ice are gifts from the lake spirits, rewarding those who brave the winter cold. Sharing your catch with friends is said to bring good luck for the coming year.

Local saying:
「冬の塘路湖、氷の下に幸あり」
Translation:
"In winter’s Lake Toro, happiness lies beneath the ice."

Smelt Ice Fishing Info

  • Location: Lake Toro, Kushiro Shitsugen National Park, Hokkaido, Japan
  • Season: Late January–Early March (varies by ice conditions)
  • Admission Fee: Free entry (fishing/gear rental extra)

  • Notes: Dress warmly; check ice conditions and availability in advance.
