Shirakawa Village

Overview

Shirakawa Village is a picturesque village nestled in the mountains of Gifu Prefecture, Japan. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is renowned for its traditional Gassho-zukuri farmhouses, which have steeply-pitched thatched roofs resembling hands in prayer. Visitors can immerse themselves in the rich history and culture of the village, surrounded by stunning natural landscapes throughout the seasons.

Visitor Information

  • Location: Gifu Prefecture, Japan
  • Recommended Season: Winter for snow-covered landscapes, or spring and autumn for pleasant weather and vibrant foliage
  • Notes: Access to the village may be limited in winter due to snow. Plan ahead and dress warmly during colder months.
  • Accommodation: Traditional ryokans and guesthouses offer a unique stay experience in the village.
