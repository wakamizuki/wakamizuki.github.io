Kottaro Marsh Observation Tower – Serene Views of Kushiro Wetlands

Overview: A Hidden Gem in Kushiro Shitsugen

Kottaro Marsh Observation Tower (コッタロ湿原展望台) is a quiet, lesser-known viewpoint in Kushiro Shitsugen National Park. From its wooden deck, you can gaze over untouched marshlands, winding rivers, and endless skies. It’s perfect for those seeking solitude, birdwatching, and a true sense of Hokkaido’s wild nature.

What Makes Kottaro Special?

Unlike the more famous Hosooka Observatory, Kottaro offers a peaceful, uncrowded experience. The observation tower is surrounded by pristine marsh, with few signs of human development. Early morning and dusk are especially beautiful, with mist drifting over the wetlands and birds calling in the distance.

  • Unspoiled panoramic views of Kottaro Marsh
  • Excellent birdwatching (cranes, eagles, waterfowl)
  • Very quiet—often you’ll have the place to yourself
  • No facilities—bring your own food and drinks

Local Legend: The Marsh’s Whisper

Local stories say the marsh is home to gentle spirits who protect the rare birds and plants. It’s said that if you listen quietly at dawn, you can hear the “whisper of the marsh” carried on the wind.

Local saying:
「コッタロ湿原のささやきは自然の恵み」
Translation:
"The whisper of Kottaro Marsh is nature’s blessing."

From JR Toro Station, the Kottaro Marsh Observation Tower is about 7 km away. Taxis are available by reservation, or you can enjoy a long walk through the countryside. Rental cars are the most convenient option.
Tip: Bring snacks, drinks, and binoculars for birdwatching.

Note: There are no facilities at the observation tower. The nearest restrooms are at Toro Station or roadside stations.

Activity Information

  • Location: Kottaro Marsh Observation Tower, Kushiro Shitsugen National Park, Hokkaido, Japan
  • Opening Hours: Open 24 hours (no lighting at night)
  • Recommended Season: Spring–autumn (birdwatching, photography), winter (crane watching)
  • Admission Fee: Free
  • Facilities: None at the tower; parking available
  • Notes: No shops or restrooms; bring your own supplies.
