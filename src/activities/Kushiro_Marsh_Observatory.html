Kushiro Marsh Observatory – Panoramic Views of Kushiro Wetlands

Overview: The Iconic Vantage Point in Kushiro Shitsugen

Kushiro Marsh Observatory (釧路市湿原展望台) is a landmark viewpoint in Kushiro Shitsugen National Park, offering breathtaking vistas of Japan’s largest marshland. The observatory features a unique building inspired by a crane in flight, and its observation deck provides sweeping views of the wetlands, winding rivers, and distant mountains.

What Makes Kushiro Marsh Observatory Special?

The observatory’s modern structure houses a visitor center with interactive exhibits about the marsh’s ecology and wildlife. The rooftop deck is perfect for panoramic photos, especially at sunrise and sunset. Nature trails start from the observatory, allowing visitors to explore the marsh up close.

  • Iconic architecture inspired by the red-crowned crane
  • Stunning panoramic views of Kushiro Marsh
  • Interactive exhibits and educational displays
  • Nature trails for hiking and birdwatching
  • Café, gift shop, and restrooms available

Local Legend: The Spirit of the Marsh

Local folklore says the marsh is protected by the spirit of the red-crowned crane, a symbol of longevity and happiness. The observatory’s design pays homage to this majestic bird, and visitors often spot cranes from the observation deck, especially in winter.

Local saying:
「湿原のタンチョウは長寿と幸運の象徴」
Translation:
"The cranes of the marsh are symbols of longevity and good fortune."

From JR Kushiro Station, take the Akan Bus (Tsurui Line, bound for Green Park Tsurui) or a taxi directly to the observatory. The observatory is located on a hill, with a short walk from the parking area.
Tip: The observatory is a great starting point for hiking the marsh’s nature trails.

Note: The observatory charges a small entrance fee for the exhibition area and rooftop deck.

Nature Trails (From Observatory into the Marsh)

Several walking trails begin at the observatory, including the Satellite Observatory Trail (約2.5km round trip), offering close-up views of the marsh and opportunities for birdwatching and photography. Trails are well-maintained and suitable for all ages.
