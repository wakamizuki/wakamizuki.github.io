Arakurayama Sengen Park – A famous cherry blossom spot with Mt. Fuji views and cultural heritage

Overview

Arakurayama Sengen Park, located in Fujiyoshida, Yamanashi, is one of Japan’s most iconic cherry blossom viewing spots. The park is renowned for its breathtaking panoramic views of Mt. Fuji, especially when framed by cherry blossoms and the striking Chureito Pagoda. In spring, thousands of sakura trees bloom, attracting visitors from all over the world. The park is also a place of cultural significance, featuring shrines and the famous five-storied pagoda.

Access to Arakurayama Sengen Park by TRAIN (Last updated: May 12, 2025)

For international visitors, taking the train is the most convenient way to reach Arakurayama Sengen Park. From Tokyo, take the JR Chuo Line to Otsuki Station, then transfer to the Fujikyu Railway and get off at Shimoyoshida Station. The park entrance is about a 10-minute walk from the station, and the climb to the Chureito Pagoda involves about 400 steps.

Access to Arakurayama Sengen Park by BUS from Shinjuku Expressway Bus Terminal (Last updated: May 12, 2025)

You can also take a highway bus from Busta Shinjuku (Shinjuku Expressway Bus Terminal) bound for "Chuo-do Shimoyoshida."
Please note that advance reservations are required for this bus, so be sure to book your seat before boarding.
From the "Chuo-do Shimoyoshida" bus stop, it is about a 15-minute walk to the park.
For details and timetables, please check the official website or the bus company's information.

Activity Information

  • Location: Fujiyoshida, Yamanashi, Japan
  • Season: Best time to visit is during cherry blossom season in spring (early to mid-April)
  • Recommended Time: Early morning or late afternoon for the best views of Mt. Fuji
  • Notes: The park can get crowded during peak cherry blossom season. There are about 400 steps to the pagoda. Bring a camera for stunning photo opportunities.
  • Entry Fee: Free admission to the park
