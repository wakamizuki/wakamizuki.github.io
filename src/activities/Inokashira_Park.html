Inokashira Park – A Serene Urban Oasis

Overview

Inokashira Park is a beloved green space in Tokyo's Musashino and Mitaka areas, famous for its tranquil pond, cherry blossoms, and relaxed atmosphere. Visitors can enjoy boat rides, strolls under sakura trees, and nearby attractions like the Ghibli Museum and Inokashira Zoo.

Access to Inokashira Park by TRAIN

The easiest way to reach Inokashira Park is by train. From Shinjuku or Tokyo Station, take the JR Chuo Line to Kichijoji Station. The park is just a 5-minute walk from the south exit. The Keio Inokashira Line also stops at Kichijoji and Inokashira-koen stations, both convenient for park access.

How to Get to Inokashira Park from Major Tokyo Stations

Departure Station Route Estimated Time Estimated Cost
Shinjuku JR Chuo Line (Rapid) to Kichijoji Station About 15–20 min ¥220
Tokyo Station JR Chuo Line (Rapid) to Kichijoji Station About 30 min ¥400
Shibuya Keio Inokashira Line to Inokashira-koen or Kichijoji Station About 20–25 min ¥200

Kichijoji Station is the main gateway to Inokashira Park. From the south exit, follow signs to the park entrance. The Keio Inokashira Line's Inokashira-koen Station is also just steps from the park's west side.

Activity Information

  • Location: Musashino and Mitaka, Tokyo, Japan
  • Time: Open year-round; facilities like the zoo open 9:30 AM–5:00 PM
  • Recommended Season: Spring for cherry blossoms, Autumn for foliage
  • Notes: Try a boat ride or visit the Ghibli Museum. Check the official website for event updates.
  • Budget: Park entrance is free; boat rides ¥700/30 min; zoo and museum have separate fees
