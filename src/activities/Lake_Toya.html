Lake Toya (Toya-ko) – Hokkaido’s Volcanic Lake Retreat

Overview: A Caldera Lake with Hot Springs and Volcanic Scenery

Lake Toya (洞爺湖, Tōya-ko) is a stunning caldera lake in southwestern Hokkaido, surrounded by mountains and volcanic landscapes. The lake is famous for its crystal-clear waters, scenic cruises, and lakeside hot spring resorts. Mount Usu, an active volcano, rises nearby, offering panoramic views and hiking trails. The area is also known for its long-running summer fireworks festival and outdoor activities such as cycling, canoeing, and nature walks. For more ideas on what to see nearby, check out the "Find Recommended Sightseeing Spots Near Lake Toya" section below!

History and Cultural Significance

Lake Toya was formed by a volcanic eruption over 100,000 years ago. The area has long been cherished for its natural beauty and healing hot springs. The indigenous Ainu people regarded the lake as sacred. Today, Toyako Onsen town welcomes visitors with lakeside baths and local hospitality. The region’s volcanic activity is showcased at the Mount Usu Geopark, a UNESCO Global Geopark.

Local Legend: The Dragon of Lake Toya

According to local folklore, a dragon spirit is said to dwell in the depths of Lake Toya, protecting the area and ensuring bountiful waters. Festivals and rituals honoring the lake’s guardian are held throughout the year.

Local saying:
「湖の龍が見守る洞爺湖」
Translation:
"Lake Toya is watched over by the dragon of the lake."

How to Get to Lake Toya (Last updated: June 11 , 2025)

Departure Station Route Estimated Time Estimated Cost
Sapporo Station JR Limited Express(HOKUTO) to Toya Station → Donan Bus to Toyako Onsen (get off at final stop) About 2 hr 20 min ¥4,800
New Chitose Airport JR Rapid Airport train to Minami-Chitose → JR Limited Express to Toya Station → Bus as above About 2 h 30 min ¥4,300~¥6,000
Hakodate JR Limited Express HOKUTO to Toya Station → Donan Bus to Toyako Onsen About 2 hr 30 min ¥5,500~¥6,500

From Toya Station, take a Donan Bus bound for Toyako Onsen. The bus ride takes about 20 minutes and drops you at the lakeside hot spring town.
Tip: Get off at Toyako Onsen for easy access to hotels, the lakefront, and sightseeing cruises.

Access on Foot (From Toya Station to Lake Toya)

Walking from Toya Station to the lake area is possible (about 9 km, 2 hours), but the bus is much more convenient, especially with luggage.

Once in Toyako Onsen, all major attractions, hotels, and the lakeshore are easily accessible on foot.

Exploring the lakeside promenade is highly recommended for enjoying the scenery, local shops, and the famous fireworks in summer.
