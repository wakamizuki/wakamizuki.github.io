Asahiyama Zoo – Hokkaido’s Top Wildlife Experience

Overview: Japan’s Most Innovative Zoo

Asahiyama Zoo (旭山動物園), located in Asahikawa, Hokkaido, is one of Japan’s most popular and innovative zoos. Renowned for its creative animal enclosures and interactive exhibits, the zoo offers visitors a chance to observe animals up close and from unique perspectives. Highlights include the famous penguin walk in winter, glass tunnels through the penguin and seal pools, and domed viewing areas in the polar bear and wolf enclosures. Asahiyama Zoo is a must-visit for families, animal lovers, and anyone seeking a memorable wildlife experience in Japan’s northern countryside. Unique Features and Animal Encounters

Asahiyama Zoo is famous for its “behavioral exhibits,” designed to showcase animals’ natural movements and behaviors. The penguin parade in winter lets visitors watch penguins waddle through the snow, while glass tunnels and domes bring you face-to-face with seals, polar bears, and other animals. The zoo’s approach has inspired other zoos across Japan and continues to delight visitors of all ages.

A Place of Learning and Conservation

The zoo is dedicated to education and conservation, offering informative displays and interactive programs for children and adults. Seasonal events, animal feeding times, and behind-the-scenes tours provide deeper insights into wildlife and environmental stewardship.

Departure Station Route Estimated Time Estimated Cost

Sapporo Station JR Limited Express to Asahikawa Station (about 1.5 hours), then transfer to the Asahiyama Zoo Line bus as above. About 2.5 hr ¥5,500 (one way)
New Chitose Airport JR Rapid Airport train to Sapporo Station → JR Limited Express to Asahikawa Station → Asahiyama Zoo Line bus. About 3 hr 30 min ¥6,500 (one way)

From Asahikawa Station, the Asahiyama Zoo Line bus departs regularly and is the most convenient way to reach the zoo.

Access by Car

Asahiyama Zoo is about 30 minutes by car from central Asahikawa. Ample parking is available (parking fee: ¥500).

The drive offers scenic views of Hokkaido’s countryside, especially beautiful in winter and spring.
