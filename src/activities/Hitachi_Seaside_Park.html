Hitachi Seaside Park – A Floral Wonderland

Overview

Hitachi Seaside Park is a vast national park in Hitachinaka City, Ibaraki Prefecture, famous for its breathtaking seasonal flower displays. The park is especially renowned for its blue nemophila in spring and red kochia in autumn. Visitors can enjoy cycling paths, an amusement park, and a variety of family-friendly attractions across expansive grounds.

Access to Hitachi Seaside Park by TRAIN (Last updated: May 12, 2025)

The easiest way to reach Hitachi Seaside Park is by train. From Tokyo (Ueno Station), take the JR Joban Line to Katsuta Station. From Katsuta, local buses run directly to the park entrance in about 15 minutes.

Note: For Limited Express trains (such as Tokiwa or Hitachi), you need a limited express ticket (tokkyu-ken) in addition to a regular train ticket. These can be purchased at ticket counters or machines at major JR stations.

From Katsuta Station, take the Ibaraki Kotsu bus bound for "Kaihin Koen" (海浜公園西口 or 南口). The park is well signposted and buses are frequent during flower seasons.

Activity Information

  • Location: Hitachinaka, Ibaraki Prefecture
  • Time: Open from 9:30 AM to 5:00 PM (hours may vary seasonally)
  • Recommended Season: Spring and Autumn for the best floral displays and pleasant weather
  • Notes: Check the park's website or local weather forecast before visiting. Some attractions and activities might be affected by weather conditions.
  • Budget: Approximately ¥450 to ¥600 per person for entry. Additional costs for rentals and activities.
